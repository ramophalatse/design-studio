Blockers

External Dependency Vulnerability

The single most consistent cause of project distress across 2025 and Q1 2026 is unmanaged external dependencies. The team has no visible escalation or unblocking mechanism — projects simply wait, and the tracker fills with "awaiting," "TBC," or "SAME FEEDBACK."

Proposed dependency escalation rule
If a project is blocked by an external or senior internal party for two consecutive fortnights, it triggers one of two actions: a formal escalation, or an explicit decision to pause and reallocate the team's capacity.
Patterns

Completion Pattern: What the Team Does Well

Every completion in 2025 and Q1 2026 shared the same characteristics. The priority projects need to be structured to behave more like these.

Time-bound with a clear end date
Single, named internal lead with accountability
Defined deliverable with obvious completion criteria
Minimal external dependencies, or dependencies resolved early
Scope contained to what the team controls

Examples of successful completions:

Pay-as-you-Drive MVNO PPB Arizona Phase 2 Repository Update CCD Masterclass Reg Design Thinking Masterclass Credit Modernisation Workshop
2026 Actions

Key Recommendations for 2026

1
Shelved projects — closed
Nedbank Connect Commercialisation and Lifestyle Commercialisation have been formally retired. NIC Digital Adoption was completed in 2025. These will not be picked up unless explicitly requested. No further action required.
2
Redistribute project accountability
Ramo currently holds 8 projects. Against the 1-project-per-lead principle, 4–5 should be redistributed. Thabo and Katlego have capacity now. Vikee comes online from May. Even partial redistribution of below-the-waterline projects would free meaningful bandwidth.
3
Unblock uMastandi
This project is not stalled by team effort or lack of interest — it is waiting on a specific internal commitment from Terry. A direct conversation is the unblocking action.
4
Split UCT Student Lounge into two explicit projects
Innovation Fund (commercial, longer-term, currently June target) and Youth X Studio (brand, active) have different owners, timelines, and success measures. They should be tracked separately.
5
Ban "SAME FEEDBACK" and "As per previous"
If nothing changed, the update must explain why and what is being done about it. A silent carry-forward signals a project that is not being actively managed.
6
Introduce a dependency escalation rule
Two consecutive fortnights blocked by an external or senior internal party triggers a mandatory decision: escalate formally, or pause and reallocate.
7
Define "done" before priority projects begin
Every above-the-waterline project should have a clearly stated completion criterion agreed upfront — the equivalent of "workshop facilitated" or "tender submitted" for strategic work.
8
Structure Nyakallo's growth intentionally
Rather than ad hoc support, define experience-appropriate deliverables per project that build her capability in a deliberate, progressive way.
9
Plan Vikee's onboarding into the portfolio
She returns from leave 1 May. Her first lead project (AI-assisted research positioning paper) is scoped and ready. Use the period before her leave to ensure a strong brief is in place.
